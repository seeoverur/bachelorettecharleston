Why Full Nudity & Alcohol Don't Mix in Charleston

This article explains the specific South Carolina statutes that prohibit the "Magic Mike" style events often advertised by out-of-state promoters.

If you see an event advertising "Full Bar available!" alongside "Full Frontal Nudity!", you are likely looking at a deception or an event that will be shut down before it begins.

The "Impossibility" Statute: S.C. Code Regs. 7-190

South Carolina maintains some of the strictest alcohol enforcement laws in the country regarding adult entertainment. Under S.C. Regulation 7-190 and SC Code § 61-4-580, the State Department of Revenue (SCDOR) strictly prohibits holders of liquor licenses from permitting "lewd, immoral, or improper entertainment."

📜 The Letter of the Law

"No holder of a permit... may knowingly... permit lewd, immoral, or improper entertainment, conduct, or practices. This includes... entertainment where a person is in a state of undress so as to expose the human male or female genitals, pubic area, or buttocks cavity with less than a full opaque covering."

- SC Code § 61-4-580(4)

What This Means for You

Legitimate venues in Charleston (bars, clubs, restaurants) rely on their liquor licenses to survive. They will not risk a mandatory license suspension to host a one-night pop-up strip show. Therefore, one of two things is happening with these online ads:

  1. The "Bait & Switch": The event will happen, but the performers will not be fully nude (stripping down to underwear only) to comply with the law, despite what the ad promised.
  2. The "Ghost Event": The venue has no idea the event is being advertised, and the promoter will cancel or "move" the event at the last minute.

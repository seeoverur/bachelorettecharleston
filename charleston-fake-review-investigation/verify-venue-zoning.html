Does That Venue Even Exist? Checking the Zoning Map.

Male revues are classified as "Sexually Oriented Businesses" (SOBs) and are restricted to specific Industrial zones. Here is how to check if a venue is lying to you.

The 3-Step Verification Test

1 Identify the Address

If the ticket website lists "TBA" or "Secret Location," stop immediately. This is the #1 red flag of a deception. Legitimate venues advertise their location.

2 Check the Zone

Go to the City of Charleston GIS Map or the "Zoneomics" database.

  • Permitted Zones: Usually Heavy Industrial (M-2) or Light Industrial (M-1).
  • Prohibited Zones: General Business (GB), Commercial (C), and all Residential (R) zones.
3 The "Church Test"

Charleston ordinances require SOBs to be a specific distance (often 1000ft) away from schools, churches, and parks. Open Google Maps. Is there a church within 2 blocks? If yes, a strip club cannot legally exist there.

Summary

If a promoter claims to be hosting a wild, full-nudity male revue at a boutique hotel on King Street (surrounded by churches and shops), they are selling you a lie. The zoning map doesn't lie.
